The Great Continent of Catonor

Somewhere in the universe, nestled in a solar system orbiting a yellow star, a blue planet containing vast oceans and lush green lands plays host to the lonely continent of Catonor.

Ecology

Sitting on the northern edge of the equator, Catonor enjoys pleasant weather along most of its land. As one travels north, the climates turns to a cold landscape of pine and mountain ranges, finally ending in an icy tundra to the northern extreme.

Origins

Catonor is a young continent when compared to the ancient civilizations of Golarion, with it's recorded history only reaching back approximately 1000 years. How it's people came to be on this planet is a mystery lost to time, but Archeologists of the present work tirelessly to uncover the lands secrets.

Tassalyr(click to show/hide)

A thousand years ago, three elven tribes roamed the varied landscape of what would later become the Kingdom. The elves were attuned to nature and enjoyed abundant sources of food nearby a great lake, and their early society was built on a deep respect for the land.


Legends speak of a visionary who united the tribes under a single banner; the first monarch Queen Aeloria. Previously a leader of one of the three tribes, Aeloria saw the explosive growth of the southern territories and feared their way of life could be swallowed by their expansion. To prevent their lands from shrinking, Aeloria declared herself queen, laying the foundations for the newborn kingdom of Tassalyr.


In the present day, Tassalyr stands as a testament to the resilience and ingenuity of its people. The monarchy, now led by Aeloria's grand-daughter Queen Eris, has embraced both tradition and progress. The kingdom continues to thrive, with its cities buzzing with life and its culture as vibrant as ever. Tassalyr remains a land of quiet beauty, where magic is subtly woven into the fabric of existence, and the legacy of its late ruler endures.

Saldaer(click to show/hide)

A thousand years ago, Saldaer was a spatttering of individual territories governed by their respective rulers, who found central Catonor to be filled to the brim with exotic spices, materials, and a varied climate allowing the cultivation of countless crops.


Seeing the potential to dominate the continent through trade, these territories combined their lands to form the Saldaer of the present, forming a ruling body of governors to run the newly born nation.

Alormyr(click to show/hide)

Early records of alormyr paint the nation very similarly to how it appears today. The church asserts that it is by divine providence that it's people were blessed with the fertile land they call home, attributing their lush forests and easy access to fresh water and food as a blessing by the Lady of Valor


Under the Light of the Sword, Alormyr grew into the great theocratic nation it is known as today. In the present day, It is governed by a religious leader or "Pope," who is chosen by the Lady of Valor and a committee of cardinals.


The Discovered World

Since the nations of Catonor are still young, the three great nations sights remain steadily focused on cultivating the relative peace each of them enjoy in the present day. As an unfortunate side-effect, the seas surrounding Catonor are largely unknown, save for a collection of Islands to the West of Alormyr, known as the Forbidden Isles.

Chronology of Events

0 PR (Post-Records): Earliest point in recorded History.

- Tassalyr is not yet formed and it's domain is ruled by three wandering elven tribes. The would-be Queen Aeloria is the leader of the largest of the tribes.

- Saldaer is a collection of city states that thrives via trade amongst themselves. Occasionally trades with the Elven tribes to the North.

- Alormyr is a budding Theocracy that enforces limited contact with outside nations. Its ruler is an Archbishop of Iomedae.

- The dominion of Frost is a solitary territory, and refuses all contact with other civilizations.

229 PR

- Saldaer unites the city states under democratic rule in order to strengthen their central position on Catonor and cement their future as a trading superpower. The citizenry elect nine councillors to govern the newborn nation. Territory grows exponentially as it's influence increases. The capital city of Nashonde is established to the north of their lands.

- Alormyr builds the holy city of Alormyr.


238 PR

- Aeloria of the Elven tribes becomes concerned that their land will be swallowed up by Saldaers explosive growth. In a bid to stake her claim to her home she declares herself queen of the northern lands and the kingdom of Tassalyr, named for her tribe.

- The remaining Elven tribes see the merit of strength in numbers and are impressed by Aeloria's audacity, and agree to assimilate into Tassalyr.

- Saldaer establishes trade relations with Alormyr.

- The Dominion of Frost refuses all contact with other civilizations.


240 PR

- The Tassalyrean capital city of Camais is built.


242 PR

- Saldaer officially acknowledges Tassalyr's claim to the northern lands and extends an offer to establish a trade relationship.


310 PR

- Tassalyr flourishes under Queen Aeloria's rule and enters a golden age.

- Tensions between Alormyr and Saldaer begin to rise due to ideological differences.


338 PR

- The Aiudara are discovered throughout Tassalyr and Saldaer.

- Princess Elaria of Tassalyr is born. A full blooded Elf. Aeloria hadn't taken a consort publicly, so rumours are spread regarding who the father might be.

- The Dominion of Frost refuses all contact with other civilizations.


350 PR

- Alormyr rescinds trade concessions to Saldaer and closes their borders, citing that "the light of the sword cannot abide a nation of faithless heathens."

- Rumours of Princess Elaria's father reach a general consensus. Many believe that an Elven councillor of Saldaer is secretly Queen Aeloria's consort.


363 PR

- Alormyr declares a holy crusade against Saldaer, aiming to spread compulsory worship of Iomedae by conquest. The Last Holy War begins.


364 PR

- Tassalyr Joins forces with Saldaer to defend against Alormyr's crusade.


380 PR

- The vicious crusade ends during the Battle of the Dry Sea. Alormyr retreats behind a wall of mountains and closes its doors to the world.

- Queen Aeloria and the Elven Councillor of Saldaer are discovered dead in eachothers arms, confirming their relationship in their final moments.


381 PR

- Lover's Quay is named to immortalise the tragic romance of the late ruler of Tassalyr and a councillor of Saldaer.


382 PR

- Saldaer begins rebuilding efforts to repair the damage caused by the Last Holy War, with assistance from Tassalyr.


386 PR

- The coronation of Queen Elaria is interrupted by an attack by Elven Separatists seeking to return to a nomadic way of life. The Tassalyrean Civil War begins.

- Saldaer lends aid to Tassalyr in order to defend the monarchy.


388 PR

- Tassalyr requests aid from the Dominion of Frost as the death toll rises.

- The Dominion of Frost refuses all contact with other civilizations.


390 PR

- The resistance is stamped out and the Tassalyrean Monarchy is restored.

- Princess Elaria is crowned Queen of Tassalyr.


400 PR

- Tassalyr slowly rebuilds from the devastation of the civil war, and enters an age of quiet reflection under the rule of Queen Elaria.

- Saldaer continues its prosperous relationship with Tassalyr, while remaining vigilant of movement from Alormyr.


498 PR

- Queen Elaria takes an Elven nobleman as consort


501 PR

- Princess Eris is born


700 PR

- Three hundred years of peace are enjoyed by Tassalyr and Saldaer as they rebuild and thrive.

- Alormyr re-establishes a cautious trade agreement with Saldaer, with heavy restrictions in place.


763 PR

- Aberrations begin to appear from the Shrouded Coast, attacking local citizens within Saldaer.

- The aberrant watch is established to prevent further attacks.


841 PR

- Queen Elaria falls deathly ill, as does her consort.

- Both Queen Elaria and her consort succumb to their illness.

- Funerals are held for both.


842 PR

- Princess Eris is crowned Queen of Tassalyr


984 PR (current year)

A supply route between Camais and the eastern city of Hallind in Tassalyr is disrupted by Separatists. The Camais Adventurers Guild recruits five adventurers to handle the disruption.
