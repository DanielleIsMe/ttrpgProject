The Great Continent of Catonor

Somewhere in the universe, nestled in a solar system orbiting a yellow star, a blue planet containing vast oceans and lush green lands plays host to the lonely continent of Catonor.

Ecology

Sitting on the northern edge of the equator, Catonor enjoys pleasant weather along most of its land. As one travels north, the climates turns to a cold landscape of pine and mountain ranges, finally ending in an icy tundra to the northern extreme.

Origins

Catonor is a young continent when compared to the ancient civilizations of Golarion, with it's recorded history only reaching back approximately 1000 years. How it's people came to be on this planet is a mystery lost to time, but Archeologists of the present work tirelessly to uncover the lands secrets.

Tassalyr(click to show/hide)

A thousand years ago, three elven tribes roamed the varied landscape of what would later become the Kingdom. The elves were attuned to nature and enjoyed abundant sources of food nearby a great lake, and their early society was built on a deep respect for the land.


Legends speak of a visionary who united the tribes under a single banner; the first monarch Queen Aeloria. Previously a leader of one of the three tribes, Aeloria saw the explosive growth of the southern territories and feared their way of life could be swallowed by their expansion. To prevent their lands from shrinking, Aeloria declared herself queen, laying the foundations for the newborn kingdom of Tassalyr.


In the present day, Tassalyr stands as a testament to the resilience and ingenuity of its people. The monarchy, now led by Aeloria's grand-daughter Queen Eris, has embraced both tradition and progress. The kingdom continues to thrive, with its cities buzzing with life and its culture as vibrant as ever. Tassalyr remains a land of quiet beauty, where magic is subtly woven into the fabric of existence, and the legacy of its late ruler endures.

Saldaer(click to show/hide)

A thousand years ago, Saldaer was a spatttering of individual territories governed by their respective rulers, who found central Catonor to be filled to the brim with exotic spices, materials, and a varied climate allowing the cultivation of countless crops.


Seeing the potential to dominate the continent through trade, these territories combined their lands to form the Saldaer of the present, forming a ruling body of governors to run the newly born nation.

Alormyr(click to show/hide)

Early records of alormyr paint the nation very similarly to how it appears today. The church asserts that it is by divine providence that it's people were blessed with the fertile land they call home, attributing their lush forests and easy access to fresh water and food as a blessing by the Lady of Valor


Under the Light of the Sword, Alormyr grew into the great theocratic nation it is known as today. In the present day, It is governed by a religious leader or "Pope," who is chosen by the Lady of Valor and a committee of cardinals.


The Discovered World

Since the nations of Catonor are still young, the three great nations sights remain steadily focused on cultivating the relative peace each of them enjoy in the present day. As an unfortunate side-effect, the seas surrounding Catonor are largely unknown, save for a collection of Islands to the West of Alormyr, known as the Forbidden Isles.
